About Jhelum Foundation

Working at the confluence of environment, livelihoods, education, health and gender justice

Our Story

Jhelum Foundation began with a simple but urgent observation: environmental degradation and social exclusion are tightly linked. When local ecosystems fail, traditional livelihoods vanish â€” and the burden falls hardest on women, youth and marginalized communities.

We build programs that treat those links holistically. Combining ecological restoration with skills training, market-linkages and advocacy, our approach is practical, scalable and people-centred.

Our Approach

Integrated design: Projects combine ecological, social and economic interventions so environmental health and livelihoods improve together.

Partnership-led: We work with government departments, the Indian Army, private travel platforms, and local stakeholders.

Community ownership: Local residents co-design activities and manage assets to ensure sustainability.

Jhelum Foundation was established in 2015 with a clear purpose: to create sustainable, community-driven solutions for social and environmental challenges in Kashmir and beyond. We believe meaningful development must be inclusive, responsible, and collaborative. Our journey continues with an unwavering commitment to people, nature, and sustainable progress.

SA

At Jhelum Foundation, we see empowerment as the foundation of development. Our focus is on creating practical opportunities through skill development, livelihood generation, and inclusive community programs. Through partnerships and innovation, we aim to enable youth, women, and marginalized communities to become self-reliant and resilient.

Our Flagship Programs

Comprehensive initiatives designed for measurable, sustainable impact

AzmatEDal

A multi-year ecological restoration mission to restore Dal Lake's water quality, reduce pollution, and rebuild tourism-linked livelihoods for communities that depend on the lake.

Lake Restoration Tourism Water Quality

KhabeTabir

Cultural revival & handicraft livelihoods initiative that documents, preserves and commercializes traditional Kashmiri handicrafts while creating market access for artisans.

Handicrafts Cultural Heritage Market Access

Seekho Aur Kamwo

Vocational training program offering market-relevant courses in hospitality, IT, tailoring, and health support services with strong placement support and employer linkages.

Skill Training PMKVY Aligned Employment

Mai Roshin

Women's empowerment program expanding women's economic participation through tailored training, micro-enterprise grants, and community safety-net systems.

Women Empowerment Microenterprise Financial Literacy

SIPDA

Social Inclusion & Placement Driven Action ensures marginalized groups gain access to market-linked skills and job placement services with barrier reduction support.

Social Inclusion Job Placement Equity

PMKVY Initiatives

Government-aligned skill development courses providing recognized certifications that improve employability through NSDC/PMKVY standards and placement support.

Government Aligned Certification NSDC Partner
